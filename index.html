路 EVSense: A Robust and Scalable Approach to Non-Intrusive EV charging Detection

This study introduces EVSense, a deep neural network (DNN)-based model designed for non-intrusivedetection of electric vehicle (EV) charging events. EVSense enhances precision and scalability by incorporatingadvanced techniques such as federated transfer learning (FTL), model pruning, and layer normalization. Thesefeatures allow efficient deployment on resource-constrained edge devices while maintaining high detectionaccuracy. Experimental evaluations on real-world and synthetic datasets demonstrate EVSense’s superiorperformance over existing rule-based and learning-based methods, showcasing its robustness against diverseresidential load profiles and varying conditions.

Refernence: Xudong Wang, Guoming Tang, Yi Wang, Srinivasan Keshav, Yu Zhang. EVSense: A Robust and ScalableApproach to Non-Intrusive EV Charging Detection, Proc. the Thirteenth ACM International Conference onFuture Energy Systems (e-Energy ‘22), 2022.

Avaiable at: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3538637.3538860

路 Towards Real-world Deployment of NILM Systems: Challenges and Practices

Non-intrusive load monitoring (NILM), as a key load monitoring technology, can much reduce the deployment cost of traditional power sensors. Previous research has largely focused on developing cloud-exclusive NILM algorithms, which often result in high computation costs and significant service delays. To address these issues, we propose a three-tier framework to enhance the real-world applicability of NILM systems through edge-cloud collaboration. Considering the computational resources available at both the edge and cloud, we implement a lightweight NILM model at the edge and a deep learning based model at the cloud, respectively. In addition to the differential model implementations, we also design a NILM-specific deployment scheme that integrates Gunicorn and NGINX to bridge the gap between theoretical algorithms and practical applications. To verify the effectiveness of the proposed framework, we apply real-world NILM scenario settings and implement the entire process of data acquisition, model training, and system deployment. The results demonstrate that our framework can achieve high decomposition accuracy while significantly reducing the cloud workload and communication overhead under practical considerations.

Reference: Junyu Xue, Yu Zhang, Xudong Wang, Yi Wang, and Guoming Tang. “Towards Real-world Deployment of NILM Systems: Challenges and Practices.” arXiv preprint arXiv:2409.14821, 2024.

Avaiable at: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.14821

路 eSpur: Software-Defined Energy Configuration

eSpur is an innovative inteligent power distribution device designed for next-generation data centers. lt offersseamless energy optimization with key features such as plug-and-play functionality, dual power sources forenhanced relilability, and multi-machine support for efficient power distribution. Real-world tests indicate energysavings of up to 21% per day, with an average savings of approximately 10%. The device is compact, fiingstandard 1U specifications (44.5x440x384 mm), and provides user-transparent, easy-to-use solutions for smartenergy management.

路 Power Demand Reshaping Using Energy Storage for Distributed EdgeClouds

This study explores the integration of backup batteries as distributed Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESSto reduce energy costs for Edge Service Providers (ESPs). The energy costs, primarily composed of energycharges and demand charges, can be significantly mitigated through strategic BESS scheduling. The authorspropose a Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) approach combined with innovations like invalid action maskingand prioritized experience replay to optimize BESS operations under dynamic power demands. Experimentalresults, leveraging real-world edge cloud power traces, demonstrate that this method reduces peak demandcharges by up to 27% and overall energy costs by up to 13%.

Reference: Dongyu Zheng, Lei Liu, Guoming Tang, Yi Wang, Weichao Li. Power Demand Reshaping Using Energy Storagefor Distributed Edge Clouds, IEEE Transactions on Parallel and Distributed Systems (TPDS), 2024.

Avaiable at: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1109/TPDS.2023.3347774
