Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) for sustainability encompasses innovative applications aimed at addressing environmental challenges and promoting energy efficiency. Our work in this domain focuses on key areas such as Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring (NILM) for granular energy consumption analysis and Battery Energy Storage System (BESS)-based peak shaving to optimize energy usage and reduce demand charges. By integrating advanced AI techniques, these efforts contribute to sustainable energy management and carbon footprint reduction. These technologies are not standalone solutions; they are interconnected and mutually reinforcing. For instance, data from NILM can inform BESS operations by providing detailed insights into usage patterns, enabling better coordination between demand-side management and storage systems. This synergy creates a more adaptive and resilient energy ecosystem.

In summary, AI for sustainability is a promising field that addresses critical energy challenges through innovative solutions like NILM and BESS-based peak shaving. By applying advanced AI models to these areas, our work aims to contribute to a sustainable future, where energy systems are more efficient, reliable, and environmentally friendly. These efforts not only align with global goals for sustainability but also pave the way for a smarter and greener energy landscape.
